***Resume:***
- Mention iFixit tech writing thingy magic shit 
- Mention my project in stats
- Put relevant experience first, not work history
	- If you have none, try to spin your story
- Use present/past tense in the first word of your sentences
	- Makes clear about when the thing happened
- Don’t put your high school diploma in there
	- Put relevant classes, coursework, scholarships, research shit
- References should be done by request, not there by default
- Keep it to one page
- You don’t need to label your phone number/contact info
	- It’s pretty apparent as is
- ***Keep your header the same between resume and [[Cover Letter Building|CV/cover letter]]***

Design/Formatting
	- Contrast
	- Titles are **bold**, bigger, and ALL CAPS -- Separate from body text
	- Use contrasting colors
- Repetition
	- Don’t go crazy
- Alignment
- Proximity
	- Keep related things together
	- i.e Don’t line break/word wrap titles/subtitles

***Templates:***
- Don’t use the Word templates, they’re a bit overused
- Change the color palette/font to make it unique if used
- If you have your initials there, be careful
	- No AF, BM, JK
	- They have connotations beyond your name

***Graphics:***
- Use graphics to show simple statistics
	- Use a pie chart to show proficiency in a skill (50% filled = 50% mastery)
- Not to show graphic design skills, but visualize skills
	- HR is a visual learner, HR likes graphics
	- In more traditional fields, they tend to prefer non-graphic resumes
- Don’t include a photo, because of DEI/non discrimination laws
	- If you do, make it 4K HD HDR 120FPS HQ Professional
	- *Should help your case*
	- Could be detrimental if the first impressions aren’t great
	- You’re at risk at looking too young, too inexperienced

***Don’t Dos:***
- Don’t include block quotes
- Don’t put your home address
	- Use your county/city
	- Personal safety
**Skills:**
- Coding
	- C++, Java, Python
***Consubstantiation***:
- Borrowed from transubstantiation
	- When you take the sacrament and are transformed into having some christ-like qualities, and the sacrament itself becomes the body of christ (catholicism)
- Rhetorically, we want to have an affinity with a peer, so we must transform ourselves
	- Think like code switching, where you match your audience to get on their level.
- You have seconds to show this in your resume
	- This is best done visually, usually with the formatting of your resume
- *Color choice*
	- Go with the color that matches the vibe of the workplace
	- Blue for healthcare, dawg use green for IT
	- Don’t use “unique” colors, as that advertises your personal brand, not your fitness
