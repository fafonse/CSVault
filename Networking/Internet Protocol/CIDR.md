**C**lassless **I**nter**D**omain **R**outing
> Pronounced "cider"

The sort of standard format of an IP address. Formed from a *subnet* part and a *host* part.
- [[Subnet]] portion of address of arbitrary length
- [[IPV4 Address]] format: `a.b.c.d/x`, where x is # bits in subnet portion of address.
	- `d` is sometimes omitted when it's zero
- `/24` is the first 3 numbers, just cut at the octet boundary

> Learn to fill in the blanks of IP addresses, given a prefix
